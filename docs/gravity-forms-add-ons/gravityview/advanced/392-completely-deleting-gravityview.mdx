# Completely deleting GravityView



### In the WordPress Dashboard, click on Views &gt; Settings

![Screenshot of the sidebar menu in the WordPress dashboard with Views menu open and arrow pointing to Settings link.](https://i.gravitykit.com/muHKkO+)

### At the bottom of the page, click on the Uninstall GravityView button

![](https://i.gravitykit.com/4TN9WP+)

### Then click on the other Uninstall GravityView button

![](https://i.gravitykit.com/YTH9io+)

### Then confirm the pop-up that warns you about what's going to happen once again

![](https://www.gravitykit.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/then-confirm-the-pop-up-that-warns-you-about-what-s-going-to-happen-once-again.png?1479076973)

### Please take a minute to let us know why you're uninstalling GravityView!

We want GravityView to be the best possible plugin. Your feedback is vital to us improving the plugin. We would appreciate any response you're willing to share.

![](https://www.gravitykit.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/please-take-a-minute-to-let-us-know-why-you-re-uninstalling-gravityview-.png?1479076976)

### Go to the Plugins page

![](https://www.gravitykit.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/go-to-the-plugins-page.png?1479076976)

### Click the Delete link

![](https://www.gravitykit.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/click-the-delete-link.png?1479076977)

### And then approve the pop-up alert

![](https://www.gravitykit.com/wp-content/uploads/2018/01/and-then-approve-the-pop-up-alert.png?1479076978)

### And there you go: GravityView's data has been removed and the plugin files have been deleted
